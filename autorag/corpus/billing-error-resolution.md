# Billing Error Resolution (Fair Credit Billing Act)

Credit card billing errors follow the Fair Credit Billing Act (FCBA), which sits
alongside Regulation Z and has its own strict timeline.

- The cardholder must send a written billing-error notice that reaches the issuer within
  **60 days** after the first statement showing the error.
- The issuer must **acknowledge** the dispute in writing within **30 days** of receiving
  it.
- The issuer must **resolve** the billing error within **two complete billing cycles**,
  and no more than **90 days**, after receiving the notice.
- While the error is investigated, the issuer may not try to collect the disputed
  amount, report it as delinquent, or charge interest on it.
- Billing errors covered by the FCBA include charges not made by the cardholder, charges
  with the wrong amount or date, math errors, and charges for goods not delivered as
  agreed.

The FCBA process applies to **open-end credit** (credit cards). Debit-card and other
electronic transfers follow Regulation E instead.
