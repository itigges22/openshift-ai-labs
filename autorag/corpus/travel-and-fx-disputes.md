# Travel, Hotel, and Foreign-Exchange Disputes

Travel-related charges generate a distinct set of disputes around holds, currency, and
no-show fees.

- **Authorization holds** (hotels, car rental, fuel) are not final charges. A hold that
  has not settled cannot be disputed; advise the cardholder to wait until the hold drops
  off, which can take up to **30 days**.
- **Dynamic currency conversion (DCC):** if a merchant abroad charged in the
  cardholder's home currency at a poor rate without offering a choice, this is a valid
  dispute under reason code **12.5 - Incorrect Amount**.
- **No-show / cancellation fees:** a hotel may validly charge a no-show fee if its
  cancellation policy was disclosed at booking. The dispute succeeds only if the
  cardholder cancelled within the stated window and holds a cancellation number.
- **Foreign transaction fees** charged by the issuer (commonly **3%**) are a pricing
  term, not a transaction error, and are **not** disputable as a chargeback.

For point-of-sale or foreign-initiated transactions, the investigation window may extend
to **90 calendar days** provided provisional credit was already issued.
