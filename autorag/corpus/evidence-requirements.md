# Evidence Requirements for Disputes

To represent a dispute to the card network, the case file must contain specific
evidence depending on the dispute type.

- **Fraud (card absent):** the cardholder's signed fraud affidavit, the device/IP data
  from the authorization, and confirmation the card was not present. A police report is
  **not** required for amounts under **$500** but is recommended above that threshold.
- **Not received (13.1):** proof of the expected delivery date, the cardholder's
  attempt to contact the merchant, and the absence of a valid tracking delivery
  confirmation.
- **Not as described (13.3):** photographs or a written description of the discrepancy,
  and evidence the cardholder returned or attempted to return the item.
- **Credit not processed (13.6):** the merchant's written refund agreement and proof
  the refund never posted.

Every dispute file must record the cardholder's first contact date, because that date
starts the regulatory clocks. Missing the first-contact date is the single most common
reason a chargeback is rejected on technical grounds.
