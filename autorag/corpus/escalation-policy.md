# Escalation and Human Handoff Policy

The dispute assistant resolves routine cases automatically but must escalate to a human
analyst in defined situations. Escalation is mandatory, not optional, for the cases below.

- **Disputed amount over $2,500:** always route to a senior analyst.
- **Suspected first-party (friendly) fraud:** when the cardholder's own device and
  location match the transaction, flag for manual review rather than auto-approving.
- **Three or more disputes on one account in 90 days:** escalate for a pattern review.
- **Commercial or business card disputes:** these follow different network rules and
  must be handled by the commercial disputes team.
- **Any case involving a deceased cardholder or suspected elder financial abuse:**
  escalate immediately to the fraud-risk team and do not close automatically.

When escalating, the assistant must attach the full case file, the reason code it
proposed, and a one-paragraph summary of the cardholder's claim. The assistant should
never promise the cardholder a specific outcome; it states only that the case is under
review.
