# Cardholder Liability Limits

Liability for unauthorized transactions depends on how quickly the loss is reported and
which regulation applies.

## Debit cards (Regulation E)

- Reported **before** any unauthorized use: liability is **$0**.
- Reported within **2 business days** of learning of the loss/theft: capped at **$50**.
- Reported after 2 business days but within **60 days** of the statement: capped at
  **$500**.
- Reported after **60 days**: the cardholder may be liable for the **full amount** of
  transactions occurring after the 60-day window.

## Credit cards (Regulation Z)

- Maximum cardholder liability for unauthorized use is **$50**, regardless of timing.
- Most networks and issuers waive even the $50 under a **zero-liability** policy when
  the cardholder reports promptly and the account is in good standing.

Zero-liability protection does **not** apply to disputes about merchandise quality or
billing errors — those follow the dispute (chargeback) process, not the fraud-liability
rules.
