# Provisional Credit Policy

When a cardholder disputes a transaction covered by Regulation E (electronic fund
transfers, including debit card transactions), the bank must extend provisional credit
while it investigates.

- Provisional credit must be granted within **10 business days** of receiving the
  dispute (**20 business days** for new accounts open less than 30 days).
- If the investigation needs longer, the bank may take up to **45 calendar days**
  (**90 days** for point-of-sale or foreign-initiated transactions) but only if
  provisional credit has already been given.
- Provisional credit is **temporary**. If the investigation finds the transaction was
  legitimate, the credit is reversed and the cardholder is notified at least **5
  business days** before the reversal.
- Credit card disputes under Regulation Z do **not** require provisional credit, but
  the disputed amount cannot accrue interest or be treated as past due while under
  investigation.

A provisional credit is never issued for disputes filed after the applicable filing
window has closed.
