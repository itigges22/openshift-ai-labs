# Card Dispute Filing Timeframes

Cardholders must report unauthorized or disputed transactions promptly. The filing
window depends on the dispute category.

- **Unauthorized / fraud transactions:** must be reported within **60 days** of the
  statement date on which the transaction first appeared. Reports made after 60 days
  may be denied unless the cardholder can show good cause for the delay.
- **Goods or services not received:** the cardholder must wait until the **expected
  delivery date** has passed, then file within **120 days** of that expected date.
- **Defective or "not as described" merchandise:** file within **120 days** of the
  transaction date.
- **Recurring billing the cardholder cancelled:** dispute the first wrongly billed
  charge within **120 days**, and provide the cancellation confirmation.

The 60-day clock for fraud is set by Regulation E and Regulation Z and cannot be
shortened by the merchant. Provisional credit, where required, must be issued within
**10 business days** of receiving the dispute.
