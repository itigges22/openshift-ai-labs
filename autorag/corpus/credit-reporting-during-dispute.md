# Credit Reporting During an Open Dispute

How a disputed balance is reported to credit bureaus is tightly regulated, and getting
it wrong creates compliance risk.

- While a billing error is under investigation, the disputed amount must **not** be
  reported as delinquent to any credit bureau.
- The issuer may continue to report the account as long as the disputed amount is
  clearly marked as **"in dispute"** and is excluded from the past-due and
  minimum-payment calculations.
- If the cardholder still owes an **undisputed** balance, that portion must be paid on
  time; only the disputed amount is protected.
- Once the investigation closes against the cardholder, the issuer must give the
  cardholder at least the standard grace period to pay before reporting the amount as
  late.
- A cardholder who believes their credit report is wrong after a dispute can file a
  direct dispute with the bureau under the **Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)**, which
  requires the bureau to investigate within **30 days**.

The assistant must never tell a cardholder to stop paying their entire bill during a
dispute; only the specific disputed amount is shielded.
