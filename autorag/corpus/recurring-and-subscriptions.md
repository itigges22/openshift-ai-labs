# Recurring Payments and Subscription Disputes

Subscription and free-trial disputes are among the most common card complaints and have
their own handling rules.

- A cardholder must first **cancel the subscription with the merchant** and keep the
  cancellation confirmation before disputing a recurring charge.
- Once cancelled, any charge billed **after** the cancellation date can be disputed
  under reason code **13.2 — Cancelled Recurring Transaction**, within **120 days**.
- For free-trial-to-paid conversions, the dispute succeeds only if the cardholder was
  **not clearly notified** of the conversion terms. If the merchant disclosed the terms
  at sign-up, the charge is generally valid.
- The cardholder may also place a **stop-payment order** on future recurring debits;
  the bank must honor a stop-payment received at least **3 business days** before the
  scheduled transfer.

The assistant should advise cardholders to cancel first, because a dispute filed while
the subscription is still active will be rejected and the billing will continue.
